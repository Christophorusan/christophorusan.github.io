Persona LTD

Agency Buildout Dublin, Ireland · Remote

I founded Persona as a publicity agency for culture, media, technology, and crypto. It grew into a 25-person remote team working across more than five countries.

The agency built performance-driven campaigns across digital ads, radio, television, print press, and institutional media, generating 50,000+ ticket sales, 50M+ online views, and up to 3x ROI per campaign.

The work sat between culture and distribution: shaping public narratives, building demand, and moving audiences for partners including EFG, CaixaBank, BBC, ARTE TV, Institut Français, and major European festivals.

Festival Publicity 2018-2024

A selection of festivals I helped sell out and build publicity for across Europe. Many were still relying on print-heavy advertising habits; my work focused on making campaigns more profitable, sustainable, and repeatable by pairing radio, television, local media, and cultural partnerships with online ad strategies that could be measured, amplified, and reused across markets.

Market read The work started before spending: mapping the venue, audience geography, ticket velocity, historic media habits, and the gap between cultural relevance and actual demand.
Accountable campaigns Print-heavy budgets were moved into tracked online ad systems with audience testing, retargeting, and ROI discipline, then compounded through broadcast, editorial coverage, partner lists, and city media.

Rendez-vous de l'Erdre 2023

Sold out 5,000 seats for Rendez-vous de l'Erdre in Nantes, France through an integrated campaign across online ads, radio, and printed press.

Built demand locally first, then widened the campaign through radio and paid social. The work connected the festival's regional identity to conversion-focused audiences, making the campaign feel cultural while still being accountable to ticket sales.

Snape Maltings 2023

Sold out Snape Maltings, an 850-seat venue in a remote location in the UK, through online ads, BBC Radio, audience development, and campaign delivery.

The challenge was distance: a strong venue, but a harder conversion path. We used BBC Radio and audience development to create legitimacy, then paired it with targeted online ads that reached people likely to travel for a high-value cultural night.

Broadcast as distribution The goal was not visibility alone. TV and radio created authority, then those editorial moments became measurable proof points for ads, partner communications, and local press.
