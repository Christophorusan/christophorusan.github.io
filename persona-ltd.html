Persona, 2018-2024

An advertising agency I founded for culture, media, technology, and crypto. The work was simple: turn attention into measurable demand.

Agency Dublin, Ireland · Remote

Persona grew into a 25-person remote team across more than five countries, working across online ads, radio, television, print press, podcasts, and institutional media.

The agency sat between culture and distribution: shaping public narratives, building demand, and moving audiences for partners including EFG, CaixaBank, BBC, ARTE TV, Institut Français, Arweave, and major European festivals.

Across the work, campaigns generated more than 50,000 ticket sales, 50M online views, and up to 3x ROI without turning the agency into a performance-marketing template.

Festival Advertising 2018-2024

A selection of festivals I helped sell out and build advertising for across Europe. Many were still relying on print-heavy advertising habits; my work focused on making campaigns more profitable, sustainable, and repeatable by pairing radio, television, local media, and cultural partnerships with online ad strategies that could be measured, amplified, and reused across markets.

Market read The work started before spending: mapping the venue, audience geography, ticket velocity, historic media habits, and the gap between cultural relevance and actual demand.

Broadcast, Radio, TV, and Podcasts 2020-2024

Media work across live TV, radio, podcasts, and printed press: shaping appearances, production moments, interviews, and editorial coverage so public attention could become a useful distribution layer.

Media as distribution The work treated broadcast and press as more than visibility. Live TV, radio, podcasts, and print created authority, then those moments became proof points for ads, partner communications, local media, and audience development.

Venue Case Studies 2023

Accountable campaigns Print-heavy budgets were moved into tracked online ad systems with audience testing, retargeting, and ROI discipline, then compounded through broadcast, editorial coverage, partner lists, and city media.

London Jazz Festival x Southbank Centre 2023

London Jazz Festival trusted me with advertising for Royal Festival Hall at Southbank Centre, where the work prioritized measurable online ads over print and radio-heavy spend.

The campaign treated one of London's most visible cultural venues as an accountability problem: use the authority of the institution, but move budget toward channels that could be tested, repeated, and tied back to demand.

Rendez-vous de l'Erdre 2023

Sold out 5,000 seats for Rendez-vous de l'Erdre in Nantes, France through an integrated campaign across online ads, radio, and printed press.

Built demand locally first, then widened the campaign through radio and paid social. The work connected the festival's regional identity to conversion-focused audiences, making the campaign feel cultural while still being accountable to ticket sales.

Snape Maltings 2023

Sold out Snape Maltings, an 850-seat venue in a remote location in the UK, through online ads, BBC Radio, audience development, and campaign delivery.

The challenge was distance: a strong venue, but a harder conversion path. We used BBC Radio and audience development to create legitimacy, then paired it with targeted online ads that reached people likely to travel for a high-value cultural night.

Persona taught me that distribution is not a channel problem. It is a translation problem: turning a project's real value into something a specific audience can understand, trust, and act on.
